What is a Credit Card?

A credit card is a plastic card that allows you to borrow money from a bank or financial institution to make purchases or pay bills. It is a form of unsecured debt and requires you to repay the borrowed amount with interest.

    Types of Loans

    Loans are financial instruments that allow individuals and businesses to borrow money for various purposes. Here's a more detailed look at common types of loans:

    • Personal Loans: Most banks offer personal loans to their customers. These are unsecured loans used for personal expenses like paying bills or purchasing items like televisions. Applicants typically need to provide proof of assets and income. The application process is usually 1 or 2 pages, and approval or denial is quick.
      Personal loans can have higher interest rates, and their tenure is not very long. Planning your finances properly is essential if you borrow a substantial amount.
    • Credit Card Loans: Credit cards are widely accepted and convenient for making purchases, including travel. Applying for a credit card is simple, either through a provided form or online. While they offer rewards and benefits, credit cards also come with high-interest rates if you don't pay your bills on time.
    • Home Loans: Home loans are long-term loans used to buy or refinance real estate properties. They often come with longer tenures and lower interest rates, especially for applicants with good credit scores.
    • Car Loans: Car loans help finance vehicle purchases, and they are secured by the purchased vehicle. A higher credit score can lead to easier approval and lower interest rates.
    • Two-Wheeler Loans: Two-wheeler loans are used to purchase bikes or scooters. They are relatively easy to apply for, but if you don't make payments on time, the lender can repossess your two-wheeler.
    • Small Business Loans: These loans support small and medium-sized businesses for various purposes such as equipment purchases, inventory, salaries, marketing, debt payments, and business growth. Eligibility criteria vary by lender.
    • Payday Loans: Payday loans, also known as salary loans, are unsecured short-term loans requiring steady employment and income. They usually have high interest rates and require salary statements as proof of income.
